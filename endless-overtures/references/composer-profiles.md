# Composer Profiles

A starting library of voices to target. Each profile gives the levers you need to build a phantom catalogue in that composer's hand: their forms, sound, textures, signature moods, typical keys, and real catalogue abbreviation.

**This list is not closed.** If the user names a composer who isn't here — Scriabin, Brahms, Grieg, Fauré, Albéniz, Mompou, anyone — derive the same six fields from your own knowledge of their style and proceed exactly the same way.

## 1. Chopin
- **Signature forms:** nocturne, ballade, étude, prélude, mazurka, waltz, polonaise, scherzo, impromptu.
- **Instrument / sound:** solo piano; warm, vocal, pedal-rich.
- **Textures:** a bel-canto singing right-hand melody over a widely-spaced, gently rocking or arpeggiated left hand; chromatic inner voices; filigree fioritura runs.
- **Signature moods:** intimate, nocturnal, yearning, with sudden stormy middle sections.
- **Typical keys:** D-flat, A-flat, E major for warmth; C-sharp minor, B-flat minor, F minor for shadow.
- **Catalogue:** **Op.** (with No. within opus). Titles: rare — let the program note carry the image.
- **Phantom large-scale option:** a "Nocturne-Symphonie" for piano and orchestra — an alternate-timeline hybrid Chopin never wrote.

## 2. Debussy
- **Signature forms:** prélude, image, estampe, arabesque, étude.
- **Instrument / sound:** solo piano; hazy, resonant, sustain-pedal washes.
- **Textures:** parallel chords, whole-tone and pentatonic colour, pedal points, bell and water figuration, blurred edges; melody often buried mid-texture.
- **Signature moods:** dreamlike, atmospheric, impressionist; weather, water, light, half-memory.
- **Typical keys:** modal and whole-tone centers; lean on mood words over strict key feel.
- **Catalogue:** **L.** (Lesure). Titles: free and evocative, often given *after* the music ("…the title in parentheses, at the end").
- **Phantom large-scale option:** a movement from an invented orchestral "Nocturne" triptych.

## 3. Satie
- **Signature forms:** gymnopédie, gnossienne, and small idiosyncratically-titled piano pieces.
- **Instrument / sound:** solo piano; spare, dry, un-pedalled or lightly pedalled.
- **Textures:** slow even left-hand chords under a single modal melodic line; almost no ornament; deliberate stillness and repetition; unbarred, directionless calm.
- **Signature moods:** hypnotic, melancholic, gently absurd, suspended out of time.
- **Typical keys:** modal (Dorian-ish); ambiguous tonal center.
- **Catalogue:** usually **no opus** — number the form plainly ("Gnossienne No. 47") and drop the catalogue token. Titles and odd performance directions fit his world.
- **Phantom large-scale option:** generally none — keep Satie to the solo piano miniature; his whole voice is smallness.

## 4. Liszt
- **Signature forms:** étude (incl. transcendental), Hungarian rhapsody, consolation, "Années de pèlerinage" character piece, ballade.
- **Instrument / sound:** solo piano played orchestrally — full range, octaves, tremolando.
- **Textures:** virtuosic cascades, wide leaps, thunderous bass, shimmering high tremolo, sudden hush; from whisper to avalanche.
- **Signature moods:** grand, rhapsodic, devotional, demonic, pilgrim-wandering.
- **Typical keys:** wide-ranging; both radiant majors and infernal minors.
- **Catalogue:** **S.** (Searle). Titles: free and poetic.
- **Phantom large-scale option:** a movement of an invented symphonic poem or piano concerto.

## 5. Bach
- **Signature forms:** prelude & fugue, invention, sinfonia, suite movement (allemande, sarabande, gigue), chorale prelude.
- **Instrument / sound:** solo harpsichord or clavichord (or organ for chorale preludes); even, terraced, un-pedalled.
- **Textures:** independent contrapuntal voices, steady motoric motion, walking bass, suspensions resolving; clarity over colour.
- **Signature moods:** ordered, devotional, geometric, serene or gravely searching.
- **Typical keys:** the full circle of keys (well-tempered); minor for gravity, major for poise.
- **Catalogue:** **BWV**. Titles: none — bare catalogue line is most authentic; the program note carries the image.
- **Phantom large-scale option:** a single fugue, or one movement of an invented keyboard suite.

## 6. Scarlatti
- **Signature forms:** the single-movement keyboard sonata (essercizi).
- **Instrument / sound:** solo harpsichord; bright, percussive, very present.
- **Textures:** rapid runs, hand-crossings, repeated notes, acciaccatura crunches, Iberian guitar-strum and folk-dance flavour.
- **Signature moods:** brilliant, witty, sun-struck, quicksilver — occasionally a grave slow sonata.
- **Typical keys:** bright majors; piquant minors with surprise modulations.
- **Catalogue:** **K.** or **Kk.** (Kirkpatrick). Titles: none.
- **Phantom large-scale option:** none — the single-movement sonata is the whole world; pair a fast and a slow one instead.

## 7. Beethoven
- **Signature forms:** piano sonata movement, bagatelle, variations.
- **Instrument / sound:** solo piano; powerful, structural, wide dynamic swings.
- **Textures:** terse motivic cells developed obsessively; sudden sforzandi and silences; singing slow-movement cantabile; driving rhythmic engines.
- **Signature moods:** heroic, stormy, tender, searching; struggle resolving to triumph or to hushed late-style introspection.
- **Typical keys:** C minor for fate, E-flat for heroism, A-flat / E major for tenderness.
- **Catalogue:** **Op.** (and **WoO** for the unnumbered). Titles: rare — occasional nickname.
- **Phantom large-scale option:** a single movement of an invented late sonata or string quartet.

## 8. Rachmaninoff
- **Signature forms:** prélude, étude-tableau, moment musical.
- **Instrument / sound:** solo piano; lush, big-handed, bell-saturated.
- **Textures:** dense widely-spaced chords, deep resonant bass, long-breathed melody doubled in octaves, Dies irae shadows, climactic full-keyboard surges.
- **Signature moods:** brooding, nostalgic, passionate, tolling, melancholic grandeur.
- **Typical keys:** C-sharp minor, B minor, E-flat minor for darkness; D / G major for fleeting warmth.
- **Catalogue:** **Op.** Titles: rare; the famous préludes go by key.
- **Phantom large-scale option:** a slow movement of an invented piano concerto.

## 9. Ravel
- **Signature forms:** pavane, "jeux d'eau"-style water piece, miroir, sonatine movement.
- **Instrument / sound:** solo piano; precise, jewelled, clean-pedalled (or his orchestral palette if scaling up).
- **Textures:** clockwork figuration, glittering high register, exact voicing, modal and jazz-tinged harmony, controlled rather than hazy.
- **Signature moods:** elegant, poised, nostalgic, faintly mechanical, luminous.
- **Typical keys:** modal centers; clear bright colours over impressionist blur.
- **Catalogue:** **M.** (Marnat). Titles: free and evocative.
- **Phantom large-scale option:** a movement of an invented orchestral suite — Ravel the orchestrator fits this well.

## 10. Schubert
- **Signature forms:** impromptu, moment musical, "song without words"-style lyric piece, sonata movement.
- **Instrument / sound:** solo piano; warm, songful, singing.
- **Textures:** a vocal melody over a steady accompaniment pattern, like a lied without words; sudden major/minor shadow-shifts; long lyrical lines.
- **Signature moods:** tender, wandering, bittersweet, with a sudden chill of melancholy.
- **Typical keys:** A-flat, G-flat, B-flat major for warmth; C minor, A minor for the shadow.
- **Catalogue:** **D.** (Deutsch). Titles: rare for the piano pieces.
- **Phantom large-scale option:** a slow movement of an invented sonata or "Unfinished"-style symphony.
