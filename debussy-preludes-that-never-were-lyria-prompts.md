# Préludes That Never Were
*A phantom catalogue for Gemini Lyria 3.1 — Debussy's impressionism, catalogued into the thousands.*

Debussy left two books of préludes and a handful of orchestral nocturnes, then tuberculosis and the war took the rest. This catalogue is from the timeline where the fog kept rolling in — where the préludes were numbered past the thousand-mark and someone finally formalised the keys he never bothered to print. The numbers are impossible. The titles still arrive, as his always did, in parentheses at the very end.

---

## How this catalogue works

- **The title is the fiction; the prompt is the instruction.** Lyria 3.1 doesn't read an `L.` number or a key signature, so every prompt below is written to *be* the mood the title only implies.
- **Debussy is modal**, so the named key is a colour-suggestion, not a rule — the prompts lean on haze, water, light, and bells rather than functional harmony.
- **Everything stays instrumental.** Each prompt rules out voices so the piano (or, for the last one, the orchestra) keeps the floor.

## The Catalogue — Préludes

### Prélude No. 412 in D-Flat Major, L. 1,990 No. 2 — *(…La lune derrière la pluie)*
> The moon is there; you just can't see it yet.

```
Solo piano prélude, French impressionist. Slow and weightless, soft sustain-pedal haze blurring every edge. A simple veiled melody drifts above gently rolling parallel chords and a low pedal point, like moonlight behind steady rain. Dreamlike, still, faintly melancholy. Very soft dynamics, no real climax, dissolving at the end. Warm intimate piano with a little room. Instrumental only, no vocals.
```

### Prélude No. 877 in F-Sharp Major, L. 1,508 No. 4 — *(…Lanternes sur l'eau noire)*
> Small lights, moving water, no wind.

```
Solo piano prélude, French impressionist, shimmering and luminous. Rippling whole-tone figuration in the high register glitters over a quiet, swaying bass, like paper lanterns reflected on black water. Serene, hypnotic, gently iridescent. Soft and even throughout with brief swells of colour. Bell-like clarity over a pedal wash. Instrumental piano only, no vocals.
```

### Prélude No. 1,431 in B-Flat Minor, L. 2,040 No. 1 — *(…Le jardin sous la cendre)*
> Something still beautiful, after the fire.

```
Solo piano prélude, French impressionist, hushed and desolate. A bare, hesitant melody picks its way over sparse low chords and long silences — ash-grey and motionless. Modal, ambiguous, quietly grieving. Pianissimo almost throughout, with one brief darker surge that recedes. Dry and close, with faint resonance. Instrumental only, no vocals.
```

### Prélude No. 2,206 in E Major, L. 2,511 No. 3 — *(…Reflets d'un autre été)*

```
Solo piano prélude, French impressionist, warm and nostalgic. A tender pentatonic melody floats over softly pulsing parallel chords — sunlit and half-remembered, like the reflection of a summer that may not have happened. Calm, glowing, unhurried. Gentle dynamics rising once to a radiant high point, then settling. Warm resonant piano, soft pedal. Instrumental only, no vocals.
```

## Phantom Forms
*Debussy didn't only write préludes — the catalogue kept all of it.*

### Estampe No. 88 in G-Sharp Minor, L. 1,620 — *(…Pluie sur les tuiles)*
> Rain on the roof tiles, counted note by note.

```
Solo piano estampe, French impressionist tone-picture. A steady patter of soft repeated notes and trickling figuration paints rain falling on tiled roofs, with a wistful modal melody surfacing through it. Atmospheric, gently restless, picturesque. Mostly soft, swelling once as the rain thickens, then easing. Clear close piano, light pedal. Instrumental only, no vocals.
```

### Arabesque No. 14 in E-Flat Major, L. 1,205 — *(…Fil d'argent)*
> A single line, drawn slowly, that never quite closes.

```
Solo piano arabesque, French impressionist, flowing and graceful. A long curling melodic line winds in unbroken arcs over a gently rocking accompaniment, like a silver thread drawn slowly through air. Elegant, serene, lightly nostalgic. Soft even dynamics with one tender high arc. Warm intimate piano. Instrumental only, no vocals.
```

### Étude No. 1,118 in C Major, L. 2,300 No. 9 — *(…Pour les notes qui s'effacent)*
> A study in notes that disappear as you play them.

```
Solo piano étude, French impressionist, delicate and quick — a study in evaporating touches. Light rapid figures fade almost as they sound, scattered over a quiet sustained haze. Precise yet weightless, playful, faintly strange. Whisper-soft with fleeting brighter flickers. Crisp upper-register piano, subtle pedal. Instrumental only, no vocals.
```

## The Phantom Triptych
*Debussy orchestrated three Nocturnes. This timeline found a second set — and never scored the last panel until now.*

### Nocturnes (deuxième série), III. — *(Lueurs)*, L. 2,600 — for orchestra
> The third panel of a triptych that was never orchestrated. Glimmers.

```
Orchestral impressionist nocturne, slow and luminous — the third panel of a Debussy-style triptych. Muted strings shimmer in soft divided chords while flute, harp, and celesta trade glints of light over a low sustained drone; a distant horn calls and fades. Weightless, oceanic, slowly breathing, swelling to one radiant tutti glow before dissolving to a single held string chord. Lush, hazy, late-Romantic French. Instrumental only, no vocals, no percussion beyond a soft cymbal shimmer and quiet timpani.
```

## Author your own

**The naming grammar** (Debussy's own tokens, the count pushed absurdly high):

```
Prélude No. [running count, into the thousands] in [Key], L. [number] No. [within-book] — (…[evocative title])
```
Debussy printed his prélude titles in parentheses *after* the music — keep that placement; it's half the signature.

**The prompt skeleton** (fill every slot, ~45–65 words):

```
Solo piano prélude, French impressionist. [tempo + degree of haze]. [veiled melodic character] over [parallel-chord / water / bell texture]. [one atmospheric image]. [soft dynamic shape — usually no big climax]. [pedal + recording character]. Instrumental only, no vocals.
```

**Key → colour cheat-sheet** (modal, so treat as a tint, not a rule):

| Key | Colour to write into the prompt |
|---|---|
| D-flat / A-flat major | warm, veiled, moonlit |
| E / F-sharp major | luminous, iridescent, sunlit |
| C major | clear, weightless, plain light |
| C-sharp / G-sharp minor | cool, foggy, suspended |
| B-flat minor | ash-grey, desolate, still |

**Two rules of thumb for Lyria 3.1**
1. Name the *texture* (parallel chords, whole-tone ripple, distant bells), not just "dreamy."
2. Debussy rarely climaxes — tell Lyria to stay soft and *dissolve*, or it will try to build a finale he wouldn't have written.
